### 2. Thermoelectric Generator (TEG) Car
**Concordia University | Thermodynamics II (MECH 351) | 2026**

Designed, prototyped, and fabricated a custom vehicle powered strictly by thermal energy conversion via a thermoelectric generator (TEG) mapped to a DC motor drivetrain. Successfully overcame internal resistance parameters to maintain a stable 1.4V output and climb a 20° incline.

*   **Empirical Cooling & Voltage Optimization Iterations:**
    *   **Iteration 1 (Ambient Cooling):** Baseline output of 0.4V.
    *   **Iteration 2 (Ice Bowl Configuration):** Elevated output to 0.7V.
    *   **Iteration 3 (Heat Sink Integration):** Achieved 1.1V. Final design leveraged an integrated heat sink coupled to an onboard ice reservoir to reach an optimal **1.4V average baseline**.
